I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Pritzker School of Molecular Engineering at the University of Chicago, working in the laboratory of Prof. Savas Tay. My research bridges **statistical physics**, **systems biology**, and **immunology** to understand how immune cells encode, process, and transmit information. I combine microfluidics, live-cell imaging, and mathematical modeling to study the dynamics of transcription factors such as NF-κB in primary macrophages, and I develop machine learning and information-theoretic frameworks to predict cellular responses.

Before my postdoc, I completed my PhD in Physics at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), where I studied stochastic search processes, first-passage phenomena, and resetting dynamics in complex environments.
