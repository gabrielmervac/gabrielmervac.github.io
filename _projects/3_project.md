How does information encoded in primary responders propagate to neighboring cells? We are combining transcriptomics, live-cell imaging, and cytokine transport modeling to understand the **fidelity and transformation of signals across cell populations**.

This project bridges single-cell information theory with multicellular communication networks, asking whether the information content of an immune signal is preserved, amplified, or degraded as it spreads through tissue. It connects naturally to questions in statistical physics about signal propagation in noisy, heterogeneous systems.
