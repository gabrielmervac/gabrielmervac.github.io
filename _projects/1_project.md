How do macrophages detect and classify inflammatory signals? Using microfluidics and NF-κB reporter systems, combined with information-theoretic analysis, we showed that cells encode signal identity through distinct dynamic features: **amplitude-based encoding for pathogens** and **duration-based encoding for cytokines**.

Remarkably, macrophages can reliably detect and classify inflammatory signals lasting as little as **one second** — a finding that challenges the conventional view of immune signal integration and suggests highly optimized signal-processing machinery at the single-cell level.

This work combines custom microfluidic platforms for precise stimulus delivery with live-cell fluorescence microscopy and downstream computational analysis to extract mutual information between input signals and NF-κB responses.
