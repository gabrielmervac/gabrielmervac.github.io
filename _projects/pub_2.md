**Journal of Statistical Mechanics: Theory and Experiment, 2022**

This paper studies the mean first passage time (MFPT) to an absorbing target of a one-dimensional Brownian particle subject to an external confining potential of the form v(x) = k|x − x₀|ⁿ/n in a finite domain. Two geometries are investigated: in setup I, an absorbing boundary is placed at the origin and a reflective wall at x = c; in setup II, both boundaries are absorbing. The central question is how the potential stiffness k, the exponent n, and the system size affect the optimal search strategy.

In setup I with a single absorbing wall, target encounter is accelerated by increasing the potential stiffness from zero only when the domain size exceeds a critical value. Below this threshold, pure diffusion (k = 0) is optimal. As the domain size is varied, the optimal stiffness undergoes a continuous "freezing" transition from a positive value to zero — a phenomenon that holds for any n > 0 and echoes the freezing transitions previously found in stochastic resetting problems. For harmonic or higher-order potentials (n ≥ 2), the MFPT always increases with k at small k regardless of domain size or particle starting position.

When the reflective wall is replaced by a second absorbing boundary (setup II), the freezing transition in the optimal stiffness becomes discontinuous, with the phase diagram in the (x₀, n)-plane exhibiting three dynamical phases, metastability, and a triple point at (x₀/c ≈ 0.17185, n ≈ 0.39539). These results are systematically contrasted with analogous results for diffusion under optimal stochastic resetting in bounded domains, revealing key differences between confinement-based and resetting-based search optimization strategies.
