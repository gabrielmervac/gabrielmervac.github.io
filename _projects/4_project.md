During my PhD, I developed analytical and computational frameworks for **first-passage processes in fluctuating environments**. The central question: how does a random searcher — a diffusing particle, a foraging animal, a molecular motor — optimize its search strategy when the environment switches between states?

Key results include:

- Identification of **non-equilibrium steady states and phase transitions** in optimal search strategies
- Analysis of **intermittent resetting potentials** that periodically confine then release diffusing particles
- First-passage statistics for **run-and-tumble particles** seeking stochastically gated targets
- Optimal **Lévy flight** strategies for finite-lived targets (with Majumdar & Schehr, Paris-Saclay)
- First-passage properties of **Lotka–Volterra systems** with stochastic resetting

These results connect to biological search (foraging, chemotaxis), molecular diffusion, and statistical mechanics of non-equilibrium systems.
